## Background

Comprehensive disease maps — curated, SBGN-compliant Molecular Interaction
Maps (MIMs) — exist for Parkinson's disease, COVID-19, rheumatoid
arthritis, the SYSCID coverage, and most recently Sjögren's disease, but
**no equivalent curated MIM exists for systemic sclerosis (SSc)**.
Existing systems-level work on SSc is limited to co-expression networks
(Mahoney, Taroni), comorbidity networks, and targeted single-pathway
sub-networks. None are SBGN-curated nor interoperable with the Disease
Maps Project ecosystem. We address this gap with a focused first module
covering skin fibrosis in diffuse cutaneous SSc (dcSSc).

## Methods

The map was built in CellDesigner using SBGN Process Description notation
across four interconnected sub-modules: M1 IFN-I signalling, M2 TGF-β /
fibroblast → myofibroblast transition, M3 endothelial-to-mesenchymal
transition (EndoMT) and vasculopathy, and M4 IL-6 / IL-4 / IL-13 /
B-cell crosstalk. Pathway content from Reactome (interferon, TGF-β,
SMADs, PDGF, NOTCH1, IL-6) was harmonised, deduplicated across modules
(including ATP/H2O/PTPN11/CBL cofactor and shared-component collapse),
and SSc-specific entities (CXCL4, POSTN, COMP, CTGF, FRA-2, endothelin
axis, …) were added manually. Annotation followed MI2CAST (HGNC,
UniProt, PMIDs, ECO codes). Single-cell transcriptomic data from Tabib
et al. (Nat Commun 2021; GSE138669) were projected onto the map for
per-patient module-activation scoring and drug-target prioritisation
(DGIdb / Open Targets).
