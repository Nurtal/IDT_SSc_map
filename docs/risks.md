# Risk register — SSc-MIM

> Mirrors the table in [README § Risks and mitigations](../README.md#risks-and-mitigations) with one paragraph per row.
> Updated on every project status review.

## R1 — Curation slower than planned

- **Likelihood:** high.
- **Impact:** late or downgraded ACR submission.
- **Trigger:** end-of-week module-target slip ≥ 1 week, or end-of-week-7 (24 Jul) gate failure.
- **Mitigation:**
  - Weekly module-level quotas, reviewed in the standup.
  - Pre-defined scope downgrade tree (drop M3 → push to EULAR 2027 → paper-only).
  - Curator rotation: a second pair of eyes (clinical referent or co-curator) once per module.
- **Owner:** lead curator.

## R2 — Reactome → CellDesigner import incompatibilities

- **Likelihood:** medium.
- **Impact:** repeated manual harmonisation; can blow a week per module.
- **Trigger:** Reactome pilot import (Phase 1 / week 1) fails to round-trip cleanly.
- **Mitigation:**
  - De-risk in week 1 on `TGF-beta receptor signaling activates SMADs`.
  - If round-trip fails, fall back to manual recreation guided by Reactome's SBGN-PD rendering.
  - Document conversion gotchas in `docs/import_pilot.md`.
- **Owner:** lead curator.

## R3 — Late expert validation

- **Likelihood:** ~~medium~~ → **low** (2026-05-16, after pivot).
- **Impact:** at gate G2 (31 Jul, revised) — abstract framed as "preliminary, validation in progress".
- **Status:** **co-author locked** (médecine interne, ARD-published SSc collaboration). Risk reduces to a scheduling-bandwidth question (R11).
- **Mitigation:**
  - Co-authorship is locked at project start.
  - Book the kickoff + two review sessions on the co-author's calendar as soon as the brief is sent.
  - Send the review package 7 days before each session.
- **Owner:** lead curator + co-author.

## R4 — Lack of "wow factor" for an ACR clinician reviewer

- **Likelihood:** medium.
- **Impact:** abstract rejected or not selected for oral.
- **Trigger:** the overlay figure (F2) is technically correct but reads "academic" rather than clinically actionable.
- **Mitigation:**
  - Invest heavily in F2 (overlay) and F3 (drug-target table).
  - One-page rendering of F2 must answer in one glance: "which patients are inflammatory vs fibroproliferative, and what would I try clinically?"
  - Pilot the figure with a clinician outside the author list before submission.
- **Owner:** lead curator + bioinformatician.

## R5 — Still "preliminary" at submission

- **Likelihood:** low–medium.
- **Impact:** acceptable for the late-breaking track, but limits the chance of a podium.
- **Trigger:** expert validation incomplete + overlay computed only on a subset of cell types.
- **Mitigation:**
  - The late-breaking track accepts ongoing work.
  - Hard requirement: the overlay figure must be complete and signed off by the bioinformatician before submission, even if module M3 is incomplete.
- **Owner:** lead curator.

## R6 — Reproducibility regression in the analysis pipeline

- **Likelihood:** medium (new entry, not in the README table).
- **Impact:** figures cannot be regenerated by a reviewer or co-author.
- **Trigger:** notebook outputs diverging from committed versions after dependency updates.
- **Mitigation:**
  - Pin all packages in `environment.yml`.
  - Set seeds everywhere stochastic.
  - End-of-phase smoke test: re-run all `analysis/**/*.ipynb` from a clean env.
- **Owner:** bioinformatician.

## R7 — Data licensing / GEO availability

- **Likelihood:** low.
- **Impact:** rework of the overlay if the chosen GEO accession is withdrawn or repackaged.
- **Trigger:** GEO accession URL change or metadata reorganisation.
- **Mitigation:**
  - Mirror raw downloads locally and record the exact retrieval date + checksums.
  - Avoid scripts that rely on undocumented metadata field names.
- **Owner:** bioinformatician.

## R10 — Zenodo DOI propagation delay  *(new 2026-05-16)*

- **Likelihood:** low.
- **Impact:** abstract submitted without a final DOI in the references.
- **Trigger:** GitHub→Zenodo webhook fires on `git tag`; DOI normally minted within 1–2 minutes, but worst case is several hours to a day.
- **Mitigation:**
  - Tag the `v1.0` release at least **1 week before** the ACR deadline — target **24 Aug 2026** for a 22 Sep submission.
  - Pre-confirm the GitHub→Zenodo integration on the repo before the first tag.
- **Owner:** lead curator.

## R11 — Co-author bandwidth in July–August  *(new 2026-05-16 — binding constraint)*

- **Likelihood:** medium.
- **Impact:** SSc Tier-1 wiring + first review round slip past 31 Jul → delayed v1.0 tag → tighter ACR window.
- **Trigger:** vacation / hospital schedule of the co-author over the European summer.
- **Mitigation:**
  - Book the kickoff + 2 review sessions on the calendar **now**, before the summer schedule fills up.
  - Send the 1-page brief (auto-generatable from STATUS.md) ≥ 1 week before the kickoff.
  - Frame the review as a 1-h walk-through of pre-curated material, not a co-curation session — the SSc Tier-1 stubs (`curation/celldesigner/ssc_additions_template/`) and pre-filled `reaction_evidence.tsv` mean the co-author validates rather than authors.
- **Owner:** lead curator + co-author.
